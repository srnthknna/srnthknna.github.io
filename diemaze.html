Rolling a Die Through a Maze

A cube sits on a grid with the number 1 face-up. It can roll one cell at a time — up, down, left, or right — and each roll changes which numbers face up, forward, and right. The goal: reach the exit with 1 back on top, in as few rolls as possible, found with A* search. Five mazes are provided. One of them looks perfectly solvable and isn't — see if you can spot which.

How the die moves

Opposite faces always add up to 7, so tracking just three faces — top, front, right — fully describes the die's orientation. Rolling it swaps those three around. Roll it to the right, for example, and the face that was on the right comes up to the top:

One extra rule for this puzzle: the die is never allowed to come to rest showing 6 on top. Any roll that would do that is simply not a legal move — which is exactly what makes some layouts harder than they look.
